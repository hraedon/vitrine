## Judgment calls

I did not add decorative family figures to the room stages. The early rooms'
sparseness reflects the curated evidence, and four stock figures would turn a
statistical composite into what looks like one literal nuclear family. The
walkthrough remains the human-scale surface and uses paid-work, unpaid-work,
and childhood lenses rather than assigning the record to a father and mother.

I also did not add microcharts to the food placards. Their values mix pounds,
quarts, dozens, and item counts, so equal visual lengths would imply a common
unit that does not exist. Diffusion and budget facts already receive honest
projections elsewhere; duplicating those inside placards would add density,
not understanding.

The folded-budget disclosure uses `<details>` in addition to SVG `<title>`.
That makes the breakdown available on touch and from the keyboard rather than
pretending a hover interaction alone is sufficient.
