## On the project

vitrine's data layer is maturing well. The corpus grew from 446 to 456 facts this session, and the new arcs surface stories that were already in the data but invisible to visitors — the mortality revolution, the healthcare cost transformation, the collapse of apparel spending, the long rise of rent. The fact that all of this was achievable by adding `Arc` entries to `curation.py` (plus a few new facts for the ratio/apparel arcs) validates the architecture: the curation registry is the single point of editorial control, and the renderer/gate machinery handles the rest mechanically.

The `Arc` / `ArcGroup` structure is clean but has one limitation that surfaced today: the `color_role` field only supports two colors (copper/brass), extended to three (brass-deep) for the mortality group. Adding a third color required a one-line change to the renderer's `colors` dict. This is fine for now, but a future group with more than 3 members would need more colors. The design spec's "no new colors" constraint (from Plan 016) applies to essays, not corridors — but the corridor palette is still deliberately narrow (two earth tones), which is the right museum aesthetic.

## On the work done

**What went well:**
- The data was already there. The mortality, NHE, healthcare-share, and rent facts were curated in prior commits. Adding arcs was pure editorial work — pointing at existing fact ids. The gate verified every arc's fact-id resolution mechanically.
- The apparel-share arc required actual transcription work: reading CEX multi-year Excel files with openpyxl to compute all-CU apparel shares for 2000s and 2010s. The 1970s-1990s apparel shares came from the existing 4-person CU expenditure-shares facts. The population splice (4-person → all-CU at 2000s) is disclosed in the arc caveats and each fact's notes.
- The expenditure-to-income ratio facts were straightforward: the ratios were already computed and stated in the notes of the existing `cex-total-expenditures` facts. Creating dedicated facts with `quantity` = ratio made them chartable.
- The Kimi adversarial review was valuable. It caught the legend/unit overlap bug that I would have missed — the hard-coded `x = pad_l + 250` worked for 2-member groups but overlapped the third legend item in the 3-member mortality group. The fix was trivial (use dynamic `legend_x` after the loop), but finding it required actually rendering the chart and checking the SVG output.

**What was awkward:**
- The plan's phasing table listed WI-8 (expenditure-to-income ratio) as "Low — curation.py entry only," but the ratio wasn't a chartable fact — it was embedded in the notes of other facts. I had to create 5 new facts. This wasn't a problem (the user asked to enrich the data layer), but the plan's effort estimate was wrong.
- Similarly, WI-6 (apparel arc) was listed as "Medium — 3 new facts" but actually required 5 new facts (1970s-2010s, since only 2020s had a dedicated apparel-share fact). The plan assumed only 3 intermediate decades needed filling, but the arc needed all decades to have dedicated facts with `quantity` = apparel share.
- Parsing the CEX Excel files required openpyxl, which is installed in the project venv but not a committed dependency. The extracted values are transcribed into the facts; the Excel files are gitignored in `samples/`. This is the correct pattern, but worth noting for reproducibility.

**What I'm confident in:**
- All 10 new facts have verified quantities (arithmetic checked against source dollar amounts).
- The ATUS note fix is correct — 37.3% is the "Working and work-related activities" percent for 2024 from ATUS Table A-1, not the 28.9% "Socializing and communicating" percent.
- The arc fact-ids all resolve (gate-enforced).
- The legend fix works — verified in the rendered SVG output.

## Gaps to flag

- The `colors` dict in `render.py:703` now has three roles (copper, brass, brass-deep). If a future ArcGroup needs a fourth member, a fourth color must be added. There's no validation or error message for an unknown color role — it would raise a `KeyError` at render time. A named-enum or assertion would be safer, but the existing pattern doesn't have one.
- The apparel-share arc has a population splice at 2000s (4-person CUs → all CUs). The arc caveat discloses this, but the chart doesn't visually distinguish the splice point. A `plot_gaps` entry or a visual marker at the splice would be more honest, though it would overstate the discontinuity (the values are close: 5.7% for 1990s 4-person vs 4.9% for 2000s all-CU).
- The `reflections/` directory is committed to git. This is the existing convention, but it means reflections are public if the repo goes public. The AGENTS.md says "Breadcrumbs live in the agent-notes DB, not in-repo dirs" — reflections may be an exception, or this may need review before the publication flip.
- The CEX multi-year Excel files were read with openpyxl (installed in the venv, not a committed dependency). If someone needs to reproduce the apparel-share extraction, they'll need openpyxl. The extracted values are in the facts; the Excel files are in gitignored `samples/`.
