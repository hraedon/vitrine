## On the project

vitrine's mechanical provenance gate remains its strongest asset. The series
layer (Plan 010) extends the "render the gap" ethos to annual data without
multiplying the curation burden — a series is one source, one tier, one
population, and a year→value table, not 78 near-identical fact blocks. The
drift detector (invariant 9) is the new load-bearing check: it catches the
same number drifting in two places (a decade fact and its backing series).
The conservative design (skip when two series share a source, skip when
fact.price_year is absent) avoids false positives at the cost of coverage —
an honest trade.

The affordability dashboard (Plan 011) is the first surface where computed
ratios (not transcribed facts) carry the exhibit. The `data-metric-id`
attribute cleanly separates computed marks from curated facts — the
mark-coverage gate ignores them, and the footer cites the recession source.
This is the right boundary: the truth path (facts) and the computation path
(metrics) are mechanically distinct.

The INFLATE derived op (Plan 012) is the first cross-entity derivation
(a fact × a series ratio). It required threading the series dict into
`evaluate()` and `_check_derived()`, which broke the room-local boundary of
Plan 006's derived-fact model. The break is justified — INFLATE genuinely
needs a series input — but it's a precedent that future cross-entity ops
will follow. The `ComputedFact.denominator = numerator` self-reference for
INFLATE is a type-system hack; the template now branches on `op.value ==
"inflate"` to avoid displaying it, but the dataclass carries a meaningless
field. A cleaner model would make `denominator` optional.

## Gaps to flag

- **INFLATE `denominator=numerator` self-reference** — `derive.py:97`. The
  `ComputedFact` dataclass requires a `denominator: Fact` field, but INFLATE
  has no denominator fact. The self-reference is a type hack; the template
  branches on `op.value == "inflate"` to hide it. A cleaner model would make
  `denominator` optional or use a union type.

- **Drift detector wage coverage** — `check.py` cross-unit check only fires
  for `fact.basis is Basis.HOURLY`. Weekly-earnings facts sharing an hourly
  series's source are correctly skipped, but a future weekly series wouldn't
  be drift-checked against weekly facts. Documented limitation.

- **`new-car-price` series sparsity** — 6 values (decade points only). The
  ORNL TEDB annual table is not in `samples/`. If the affordability
  dashboard's car-as-hours metric uses this series, it shows 6 points. Not
  wrong, but the chart implies more density than exists.

- **`median-home-value-acs` sparsity** — 4 of ~20 possible ACS years. More
  CSVs in `samples/06-acs-csv/` would fill this. The home-as-income-years
  metric is very sparse as a result.

- **1900s anchor year gap** — wage anchor is 1908, income anchor is 1900-01.
  The 7-8 year gap is flagged by the comparator but a visitor might not
  notice. The fact notes explain it.

- **2020s internet facts labeled 2025** — `us-2020s-internet-diffusion` and
  `us-2020s-broadband-diffusion` use 2025 data while `data_as_of = "2024"`.
  Pre-existing; the plan's "up to anchor year" rule makes this visible.

- **`uv.lock` untracked** — decide whether to commit. It's a new file from
  the `uv venv && uv pip install` step.
